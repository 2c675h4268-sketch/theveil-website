What Changed

The story this week is institutional, not extraterrestrial: the White House stood up a UAP board with named scientific advisers, and that is the rare development with real weight behind it. The New York Post (76) and NBC News (74) both put Avi Loeb on the record about the panel's mandate, and NBC lets him describe a data-first standard in his own words. A government board that names its scientists and meets on the calendar is something you can actually hold to account later.

What to watch is narrow and concrete. Get the charter, the reporting line, and whether this produces public findings or quietly absorbs the issue. Michael Shermer writing in Skeptic (60) that he was appointed suggests an attempt at methodological balance, which is encouraging if dissent gets real weight.

Discount the rest. Off The Press (49) claims Loeb will lead, not just advise, with no official roster to confirm it. The Fox, Tyson, and Spielberg pieces are opinion and film promotion. The Economic Times black hole "UFO" is astrophysics jargon. Not your story.
